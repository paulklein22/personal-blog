The Moviemaking Magic of Star Wars: Creatures + Aliens

by Paul Klein, January 1, 2020
Nonfiction

Bantha

Only one elephant was used, so shots with two banthas were composed using optical effects. The only scene in A New Hope with more than one bantha is the shot where Luke is watching them through his macrobinoculars.

Dewback

George Lucas hated the final model as he thought it was too static. Maybe that’s why it was left (along with the Krayt dragon skeleton) abandoned in the Tunisian desert after production ended.

Jawas

Jawas were originally going to have rodent-like features, but they were eventually pared back to just glowing eyes in a dark hood. Jawas are one of the most recognizable Star Wars creatures so I think it’s pretty clear this was a good decision.
